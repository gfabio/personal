Games

While I am technically majoring in Digital Media at University of Rochester, I have done my utmost to turn it into a Game Design curriculum. To this end, I spent a semester in Singapore at Nanyang Technological University to study Interactive Media and game development. Listed here are the fruits of my efforts thus far.

The Rite

Note: in-browser play requires Unity Web Player, which does not run in Chrome.

I developed The Rite in Unity 3D in less than 48 hours for the Global GameJam 2016. The theme was "ritual", and I chose to play on the expectations of that word but ultimately pull away from that expectation. I had planned to go in with a team, but plans fell apart and I ended up working by myself. Assets were created with Maya 2015, GIMP, Paint Tool SAI, and Garageband. A couple friends provided images to use in the game; credits are on the GameJame site, linked below.

GameJam site/Download

Aster

I came up with the concept for Aster in March 2015, while spending my spring break hiking in the mountains of Vietnam. I continued developing it on my own until it was chosen as a senior capstone project at University of Rochester. I am the Project Manager, Creative Director, Artist, Level Designer, Game Writer, Technical Writer, and Character Modeler on the team (and various other positions as needed).

The game is built in Unreal Engine. I designed levels and drew concept art and visual assets in Paint Tool SAI and GIMP; I modeled characters in Sculptris and Maya 2015. Some art can be found here. I wrote the project plan and game script and have presented the game formally to panel members half a dozen times. As the team leader I have also kept my team organized and on track. Aster is due to be released in April 2016.

Processing

I made this very small "game" as a final project for a digital art class in 2014. To play, navigate with the arrow keys, and interact with any colored space by hitting the enter or return key. To continue, pick an option and follow that link.

Unfortunately, the embedded images in both options do not work on the OpenProcessing site, and the parts must be downloaded and opened in Processing to play. One is a maze while the other is a buzzer game-like path to follow.
